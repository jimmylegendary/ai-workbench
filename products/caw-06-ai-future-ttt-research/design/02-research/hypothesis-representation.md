## Purpose

This doc decides **how CAW-06 represents an uncertain future-AI / TTT hypothesis without overclaiming**: the
data model of a hypothesis, its **status lifecycle** (hypothesis / supported / refuted / inconclusive), how
**confidence and uncertainty** are recorded, how **evidence** is linked, and the hard rule that a hypothesis is
**never rendered as a settled claim**. It defines fields and state transitions; it is the contract the
`ExperimentScout` pipeline and exports to CAW-01 / CAW-02 must honour.

It does **not** define: the claim-extraction algorithm or source ingestion (separate doc), the experiment ledger
schema (separate doc — this doc only consumes its `verdict`), the writeback-traffic schema (separate doc), or
storage/serialization mechanics (ADR). It assumes the family data direction from the brief: markdown/JSON records,
each carrying provenance, status/uncertainty, and `boundary`.

## 1. Why this is load-bearing

Future-AI/TTT claims are easy to over- or under-claim, and the brief's guardrails are explicit: *keep sources,
claims, evidence, and generated conclusions separate; generated summaries are not evidence; a hypothesis is never
presented as a settled claim* (PRODUCT-BRIEF §12). The representation is the enforcement mechanism for those
guardrails — if the model cannot structurally distinguish "a paper says X", "we generated hypothesis Y", and "our
toy experiment supports Y", the product leaks overclaim into its exports to CAW-01 and CAW-02.

The field's volatility makes this concrete. TTT/test-time-compute is a moving target: methods range from
test-time *scaling* (frozen weights, sample-and-select) to test-time *training* (gradient updates to "fast
weights" during inference) — and which variants actually **write back** is itself unverified (PRODUCT-BRIEF §6).
A representation that bakes in "TTT writes back" as fact would corrupt the CAW-01 workload-axis bridge. So the
**writeback hypothesis is a tracked `Hypothesis`, not a premise.**

## 2. The three separated layers (anti-overclaim core)

The representation keeps three record kinds **distinct and separately addressable**. They reference each other by
id; they are never merged into one "fact" blob. This mirrors evidence/assertion ontologies that separate the
*assertion*, the *evidence* for it, and its *provenance* (see Sources).

| Layer | Record kind | Truth status | Origin | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Source claim** | `Claim` | What a *source asserts* (not what WE assert) | extracted from public research | "TTT-E2E compresses context into weights via next-token prediction." |
| **Hypothesis** | `Hypothesis` | What WE propose to check — **always provisional** | generated by `ExperimentScout` | "TTT-class inference generates write traffic absent from read-dominant serving." |
| **Evidence** | `Evidence` | An observation bearing on a hypothesis | source citation OR our experiment result | toy-experiment verdict; a cited measurement |

**Hard rules.**
1. A `Hypothesis` is **never serialized without its `status`**; default and floor is `hypothesis`.
2. A **generated summary is `Evidence` of kind `generated` only, and `generated` evidence can never, on its own,
   move a status to `supported` or `refuted`** (it is not evidence per §12). It may inform `inconclusive`.
3. A `Claim` carries `asserted_by` provenance; it is rendered as *"<source> claims …"*, never as *"it is true
   that …"*. Restating a source claim as our conclusion is forbidden.
4. Exports (to CAW-01/CAW-02) carry the `status`, `confidence`, and evidence links **inline** — a hypothesis may
   not cross a product boundary stripped of its uncertainty (see §7).

## 3. Status lifecycle

Four statuses, exactly as fixed by the brief (PRODUCT-BRIEF §5.2). Status is a property of *current evidence*, not
a permanent label — it is reversible.

| Status | Meaning | Entry condition | May export as |
|---|---|---|---|
| `hypothesis` | Proposed, not yet tested | default on creation | open question / proposal only |
| `supported` | Evidence consistent with it; **not proven** | ≥1 `Evidence` of kind `experiment`/`external` with supporting verdict, above evidence bar (§4) | "supported (provisional)" claim+evidence |
| `refuted` | Evidence contradicts it | ≥1 disconfirming `Evidence` above bar | negative result (first-class) |
| `inconclusive` | Tested but evidence weak/mixed/null | experiment ran but verdict ambiguous, or conflicting evidence | open question + logged attempt |

```
                    ┌───────────────────────────────────────┐
                    v                                         │
   (create) ──► hypothesis ──experiment/external──► supported ┤
                    │  ▲                                       │ new disconfirming
                    │  │ re-opened by new/contradicting        │ evidence
                    │  │ evidence (any transition reversible)  v
                    │  └───────────────────────────── refuted ─┘
                    │
                    └── ran, weak/mixed/null ─► inconclusive ──► (re-test) ─► hypothesis
```

**Transition rules.**
- Every transition writes a `StatusEvent` (timestamp, from→to, triggering evidence ids, actor). The lifecycle is
  an **append-only log**; the current status is the latest event. This keeps failures and reversals auditable.
- `supported` and `refuted` are **never terminal** and never mean "proven/disproven" — only "current evidence
  leans this way". Re-opening on new/contradicting evidence is expected, not exceptional.
- Only `experiment` or `external` evidence (not `generated`) can drive `→ supported` / `→ refuted` (§2 rule 2).
- A hypothesis with zero evidence **cannot** be anything but `hypothesis`.
- Jimmy is the reviewer for strategic status promotions; the pipeline proposes, a human confirms `supported`
  exports (PRODUCT-BRIEF §12).

## 4. Confidence & uncertainty fields

We separate **status** (which way evidence leans) from **confidence** (how strongly), adopting the IPCC two-metric
pattern — *confidence* from evidence quantity/quality + agreement, and optional *likelihood* when quantifiable
(see Sources). We deliberately use **qualitative, calibrated** terms by default to avoid false precision.

| Field | Type | Values | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| `confidence` | enum | `very-low` \| `low` \| `medium` \| `high` \| `very-high` | derived from `evidence_strength` × `agreement`; default `very-low` |
| `evidence_strength` | enum | `none` \| `weak` \| `moderate` \| `strong` | quality+quantity of non-`generated` evidence |
| `agreement` | enum | `conflicting` \| `mixed` \| `consistent` | across evidence items |
| `likelihood` | optional enum | `unlikely` \| `about-as-likely-as-not` \| `likely` \| `very-likely` | ONLY if quantified; else omit (do not invent) |
| `uncertainty_notes` | markdown | free text | what would change our mind; known confounders |
| `falsifiability` | markdown | free text | the observation that would refute it — required to leave `hypothesis` |
| `reproducibility` | enum | `unrun` \| `single-run` \| `replicated` \| `failed-to-reproduce` | links to ledger entries |

**Rules.**
- `confidence` is **capped by `evidence_strength`**: `none → very-low` (max), `weak → low`, regardless of how
  compelling the prose seems. A hypothesis backed only by `generated` evidence is pinned at `very-low`.
- `likelihood` is **omitted, not guessed**. Empty ≠ "about as likely as not". Per DOC-CONVENTIONS, unknowns are
  `TODO(open-question: ...)`, never fabricated numbers.
- A hypothesis missing `falsifiability` is a `TODO`, not a `supported` candidate — untestable hypotheses stay
  `hypothesis` and are flagged.

## 5. Hypothesis record model

JSON-shaped (markdown front-matter or `.json`, per storage ADR). Illustrative — the builder writes the schema.

```jsonc
{
  "id": "hyp-2026-0007",
  "kind": "Hypothesis",
  "statement": "TTT-class inference (weight/state writeback during serving) produces a write-traffic profile not captured by read-dominant LLM-serving memory assumptions.",
  "theme": "ttt-writeback",                 // one of the tracked research themes
  "status": "hypothesis",                    // hypothesis | supported | refuted | inconclusive
  "confidence": "low",
  "evidence_strength": "weak",
  "agreement": "mixed",
  "likelihood": null,                        // omit unless quantified — do NOT invent
  "falsifiability": "A measured TTT variant shows write bytes/token ≈ 0 vs. baseline ⇒ refuted.",
  "uncertainty_notes": "Which TTT variants actually write back is unverified (PRODUCT-BRIEF §6).",
  "reproducibility": "single-run",
  "derived_from_claims": ["clm-2026-0031", "clm-2026-0042"],   // Claim ids (source assertions)
  "evidence": ["evd-2026-0101", "evd-2026-0118"],              // Evidence ids
  "status_log": [
    {"ts": "TODO", "from": null, "to": "hypothesis", "by": "ExperimentScout", "evidence": []},
    {"ts": "TODO", "from": "hypothesis", "to": "supported", "by": "Jimmy", "evidence": ["evd-2026-0118"]}
  ],
  "implications": ["imp-2026-0003"],         // implication-map node ids
  "boundary": {                              // export routing — never a shared store
    "exports_to": ["CAW-01:open-question", "CAW-02:claim+evidence"],
    "imports_from": ["CAW-05:signal-9921"]
  },
  "provenance": {"created_by": "ExperimentScout", "created_at": "TODO", "review_state": "unreviewed"}
}
```

Companion `Evidence` record (kept separate per §2):

```jsonc
{
  "id": "evd-2026-0118",
  "kind": "Evidence",
  "evidence_kind": "experiment",            // experiment | external | generated
  "supports": "hyp-2026-0007",
  "direction": "supporting",                // supporting | disconfirming | neutral
  "strength": "moderate",
  "ledger_ref": "exp-2026-0044",            // small-experiment ledger entry (incl. failures)
  "source_ref": null,                       // set for evidence_kind=external (citation)
  "note": "Toy reproduction measured non-zero write bytes/token under TTT update; single run."
}
```

## 6. Worked examples (calibration)

| Situation | status | evidence_strength | confidence | Why |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Scout generated hypothesis from 2 source claims, nothing run | `hypothesis` | `weak` | `very-low` | generated-only; capped |
| Toy reproduction shows non-zero writeback, one run | `supported` | `moderate` | `low` | single experiment; reproducibility=`single-run` |
| Two toy runs disagree | `inconclusive` | `weak` | `very-low` | `agreement=conflicting` |
| Reproduction shows ~zero write traffic for that variant | `refuted` | `moderate` | `medium` | disconfirming, matches falsifiability |
| LLM summary "strongly suggests TTT dominates memory" only | `hypothesis` | `none` | `very-low` | generated ≠ evidence (§2 rule 2) |

## 7. Boundary / export behavior

- **To CAW-01 (a separate product):** a `hypothesis`-status item exports as a **future-workload open question**
  carrying `confidence`, `uncertainty_notes`, and `falsifiability`. Only `supported` items export as candidate
  workload-axis inputs, and still flagged `provisional`. No shared store — a file/API handoff (PRODUCT-BRIEF §8).
- **To CAW-02 (a separate product):** export `Claim` + linked `Evidence` only when `status ∈ {supported,
  refuted}`; the `status` + `confidence` travel inline. Bare hypotheses do not export as knowledge.
- **From CAW-05 (a separate product):** an imported TTT radar signal opens a new `Hypothesis` at
  `status=hypothesis`, `confidence=very-low`, with the signal recorded as `external` evidence — never auto-promoted.
- Nothing crosses a boundary stripped of status/uncertainty (§2 rule 4).

## Implications for runbooks

- **Schema runbook:** define `Hypothesis`, `Claim`, `Evidence` as **three separate record types** with id
  cross-refs; enforce `status` required + default `hypothesis`; enforce `confidence ≤ evidence_strength` cap.
- **Lifecycle runbook:** implement the append-only `status_log`; implement a validator that **rejects**
  `supported`/`refuted` whose only evidence is `evidence_kind=generated`.
- **ExperimentScout runbook:** on hypothesis generation, set `status=hypothesis`, `confidence=very-low`, populate
  `derived_from_claims`; require a `falsifiability` field or emit a `TODO`.
- **Ledger integration runbook:** a ledger `verdict` (incl. failure) creates an `Evidence` record and proposes a
  `StatusEvent`; failures map to `refuted`/`inconclusive`, never silently dropped.
- **Export adapter runbooks (CAW-01/CAW-02):** carry `status`+`confidence`+evidence inline; block export of bare
  hypotheses as knowledge; tag all exported `supported` items `provisional`.
- **Rendering/CLI runbook:** any human/agent-facing rendering of a `Hypothesis` MUST display status + confidence;
  forbid templates that print a hypothesis statement as a bare assertion.
